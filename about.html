Quick Plumbing Tips from the Experts

1. Know Your Main Water Valve

Locate the main water shut-off valve for your home (usually outside or in the basement). Knowing its location and how to turn it off is critical in a burst pipe emergency to minimize water damage.

2. Never Put Grease Down the Sink

Pouring fats, oils, or grease down the kitchen sink is the number one cause of blocked drains. Always wipe greasy pots and pans with a paper towel before washing them.

3. Be Mindful of Toilet Use

Only flush toilet paper. Items like wet wipes (even "flushable" ones), feminine hygiene products, or paper towels should always be placed in a bin to prevent clogs.

4. Insulate Exposed Pipes

During cold weather, ensure any exposed pipes, especially those outside or in unheated areas, are properly insulated. This prevents freezing, which is the cause of most winter burst pipes.
